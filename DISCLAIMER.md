# QadamCare AI Disclaimer

## Educational and research use only

QadamCare AI is an educational engineering and research prototype. It is not a medical device, has not been clinically validated, and has not received regulatory approval.

## No medical diagnosis

The software does not diagnose or rule out:

- diabetes
- diabetic-foot disease or diabetic-foot ulcer
- infection
- ischemia or vascular disease
- neuropathy
- osteomyelitis
- gangrene or sepsis
- wound depth or severity
- future ulcer location
- prognosis, amputation risk, or treatment need

A dataset-defined healthy/control-like image pattern is not proof that a person is healthy. A dataset-defined diabetic-foot-like image pattern is not proof that a person has diabetes or diabetic foot.

## No treatment or emergency guidance

The software must not be used to prescribe or select medication, antibiotics, dressings, debridement, surgery, offloading, vascular intervention, or any other treatment. It must not be used for emergency triage.

Seek qualified medical care for concerning symptoms or clinical findings. The repository does not provide individual medical advice.

## User-entered information

Age, gender, diabetes history, pain, redness, swelling, warmth, discharge/odour, fever, neuropathy, vascular history, probe-to-bone findings, and previous-visit information are entered by the user or clinician. They are not automatically verified and are not necessarily inferred by the image models.

## Thermal-output limitation

Thermal images may be grayscale or pseudo-coloured. Pixel intensity and display colour are not automatically calibrated temperature measurements. Monitoring zones are relative visualisation outputs and do not predict where an ulcer will develop.

## Generative AI limitation

Local LLM/VLM sections are generated text. They may be incomplete, inconsistent, misleading, or factually wrong. They can confuse missing information with explicitly negative findings or overinterpret model outputs. Generated narratives must be checked against the structured case data and direct clinical assessment.
